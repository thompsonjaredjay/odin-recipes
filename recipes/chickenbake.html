Copycat Costco Chicken Bakes

Description

Costco's Chicken Bakes have a special place in my heart. I used to eat one every day for lunch from middle school to halfway through college. They were so convenient to pop in the microwave, wait two minutes, and BAM! flavor town. They are wonderful sandwich rolls covered with italian cheeses and stuffed with chicken, bacon and creamy Caesar dressing.

This recipe is a copycat of Kirkland's microwaveable masterpiece. Why go through all the work when you can just microwave it? I have no clue. But, you might get a sense of pride from making it yourself and you get to taste what it's like when the ingredients are not frozen.

This recipe takes 15 minutes of prep time and 45 minutes of cook time for a total of 1 hour. It yields 3 chicken bakes which can serve 6 people.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound prepared pizza dough
  • 3 skinless, boneless chicken breasts
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3/4 cup water, or more as needed
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour, or as needed for surface
  • 1/2 cup creamy Caesar dressing
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded Italian cheeses, such as mozzarella and provolone, divided
  • 1 large egg, beaten

Steps

  1. Let pizza dough rest at room temperature for about 1 hour before using
  2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  3. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil, and lay bacon strips in a single layer on the sheet
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until browned and crisp, about 15 minutes
  5. Drain bacon slices on paper towels. Crumble when cool enough to handle. Leave the oven on
  6. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and garlic powder
  7. Heat a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat
  8. Add oil and place chicken breast in the skillet, seasoned side down
  9. Cook undisturbed until golden brown, about 5 minutes
  10. Season the top side, flip, and cook 1 more minute
  11. Add just enough water to fill the bottom of the pan and remove pan from heat
  12. Bake chicken in the preheated oven until an instant read thermometer, inserted near the center, reads 165 degrees F (74 degrees C), 17 to 20 minutes
  13. Remove from heat and let stand until cool enough to handle, then chop chicken and set aside
  14. Increase oven temperature to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C), line a baking sheet with parchment
  15. Sprinkle flour onto a large work surface and divide pizza dough into 3 circular portions with 12 to 13 inch diameter
  16. Spread dressing evenly over dough circles, leaving a 1-inch border
  17. Sprinkle 2 tablespoons Parmesan cheese over dressing, reserve 2 tablespoons shredded italian cheese blend and set aside
  18. Top dough circles evenly with chicken, bacon, green onion, and remaining italian cheese blend
  19. Fold outer left and right sides slightly over the filling, roll the bottom of the dough circle over the filling and then roll towards the top edge until fully wrapped
  20. Pinch edges to seal and use hands to work into a long log shape about 12 inches long
  21. Place filled dough, seam side down, on the prepared baking sheet
  22. Brush beaten egg over tops of dough and sprinkle evenly with cheese blend and sprinkle each with 1 tablespoon shredded Parmesan cheese
  23. Bake in preheated oven until golden brown, about 20 minutes
