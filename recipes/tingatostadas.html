Chicken Tinga Tostadas

Description

This recipe is one of my two favorite tostada recipes ever. This one has a special place in my heart because it reminds me of the first time my wife and I visited a farmers market together in Utah. There was a mexican food truck and we tried a bunch of different dishes but we fell in love with the tinga tostadas instantly.

This recipe has a prep time of 20 minutes and a cook time of 1 hour for a total of 1 hour and 20 minutes. It yields 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 6 bone-in chicken breast halves
  • 7 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, chopped
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 6 medium tomatoes
  • 1 (7 ounce) can chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon granules
  • 16 tostada shells
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Place the chicken into a large pot along with 1/4 cup of sliced onion, garlic, and enough water to cover
  2. Bring to a boil, and cook until no longer pink, about 25 minutes
  3. Remove chicken, cool, and shred meat. Discard skin and bones
  4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the remaining onion slices, and fry until transparent
  5. Place tomatoes into a pan of boiling water (you could use the leftover chicken water), and boil until they split open
  6. Place the tomatoes, the whole can of chipotle peppers, chicken bouillon into a blender or large food processor. Process until smooth
  7. Season with salt and pepper
  8. Pour this mixture into a large pan, and add the shredded chicken and cooked onions. Heat through
  9. To serve, each person spreads some sour cream on their tostada shell, and tops with a spoonful of the chicken (my wife and I like to substitute the sour cream with mayonnaise)
